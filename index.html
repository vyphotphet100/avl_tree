AVL TREE

AVL tree is a self-balancing Binary Search Tree (BST) where the difference between heights of left and right subtrees cannot be more than one for all nodes.

Get Started

Rotating the subtrees in an AVL Tree

In rotation operation, the positions of the nodes of a subtree are interchanged inorder to make sure that the binary search tree is balanced

Left Rotate

“In left-rotation, the arrangement of the nodes on the right is transformed into the arrangements on the left node.”

For more information Click here!

Right Rotate

“In right-rotation, the arrangement of the nodes on the left is transformed into the arrangements on the right node.”

For more information Click here!

Left-Right and Right-Left Rotate

“In left-right rotation, the arrangements are first shifted to the left and then to the right.”

“In right-left rotation, the arrangements are first shifted to the right and then to the left.”

Algorithm AVL

In this section we will have a look at some of the basic algorithm!

Insert

Inserting a new node into AVL tree is the same as what we have early done in BST tree.

After inserting a new node, AVL tree rely heavily on balance factor of every root node to check every left and right subtree is balanced or not? If not, then proceed to balance it base on some of rotations that we have already had previewed in the previous section.

For more information Click here!

Delete

Deleting a node of AVL tree is a little bit more tricky.

After deleting a node, we have to make sure that the tree after deletion is a valid BST and also the balance factor of each node's subtree as well.
